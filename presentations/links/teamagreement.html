CSCC01 Team Expectations Agreement

When working in a team, problems occasionally arise. One source of problems is the differing expectations of team members. For example, one person may think that an email response should come within 2 hours, while another may think that 2 days is acceptable. To start off the project, your team will establish team expectations. In the space below, write down the list of agreed upon guidelines that your team intends to follow. Include guidelines for the following:

methods of communication: We will be using Facebook Messenger for our main method of communication we have made a group chat for this project, we will send all communication messages, pictures (including code, or questions about the work), files(including code, pdf's), etc using Facebook Messenger. We have a second method of communication which is Skype which can be used for video or phone calls. We will also use Slack to communicate with the TA

communication response times: We have decided response times on messenger will be within 2 hours from 9am-9pm and after that can be the next day.

regular meeting times: Our regular meeting times will be at 11:30am-12:30pm, followed by a weekly TA meeting from 12:30pm-1:15pm and again another team meeting which we can take into consideration what the TA said and our next steps for the next week will be from 1:15pm-2:00pm.

meeting attendance: We have decided, TA meetings are mandatory, our weekly meeting on Wednesdays will be mandatory unless you have an emergency. And if we have additional meetings on Thursday or Friday they can be optional.

running meetings: All mandatory meetings on Wednesday will be in person at UTSC inside IC. We have a secondary source of meetings which will be used under emergencys which can be done on Skype.

meeting preparation: We should mentally prepare what we need to talk about, problems and conflicts we had, if anything needs to be changed, etc.

version control: All substantial changes and bug fixes need to be committed immediately. If something is to be not committed we will send it via Facebook Messenger for example minutes from meetings.

division of work: We will divide work based on feature set, and allocate work based on individual strengths. We will have a weekly discusion during our meeting on what needs to be done for the coming week.

submitting work: All work will be submitted on github or facebook messenger, deliverables we will be due Saturday night as a team deadline, with a review the next day. Team due dates for code will be due Tuesday night, with a review and team meeting every Wednesday. Everyone will submit their own work to the GIT repository and memebers will rotate to review the wnds
